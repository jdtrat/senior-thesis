2 R

The work discussed in this thesis was primarily done with the programming language R. This choice was brought about because both myself and my advisor(s) were active R users. Why we chose to learn R originally, though, is explained by understanding who, and why, millions of people use R. This brief section describes R and why it’s popular.

2.1 What is R?

R is an interpreted, object-oriented language developed in the early 1990s by Ross Ihaka and Robert Gentleman(Ihaka and Gentleman 1996). It is “an integrated suite of software facilities for data manipulation, calculation and graphical display”(The R Foundation, n.d.). R is often thought of as a statistical computing language, though it may be better categorized as an “environment with which statistical techniques are implemented” (The R Foundation, n.d.). Recently, it has gained widespread popularity in both academia and industry jobs (Carson and Basiliko 2016; Ashlee Vance 2009; RStudio 2018a).

2.2 Why is R becoming more popular?

One reason for R’s rising popularity is its easy extensibility with packages, the fundamental unit of shareable code (Hadley Wickham and Jenny Bryan, n.d.). Packages bundle code, data, documentation, and tests in a common manner which allows all R users to benefit from others’ work (Hadley Wickham and Jenny Bryan, n.d.). Packages can be shared internally (e.g. within a research lab) or publicly. The most common way to publicly share your code is through the Comprehensive R Archive Network (CRAN) (“The Comprehensive R Archive Network,” n.d.). CRAN provides discoverability, facilitates easy installation, and lends a sense of authenticity (each package must pass certain standards) (Hadley Wickham and Jenny Bryan, n.d.). At the time of writing,1 CRAN has 17,313 available packages (three of which are discussed in this thesis). Packages exist for everything from implementing hierarchical Bayesian models (Bürkner et al. 2021) to image manipulation (Ooms [aut and cre 2021) and console-based games (Xie [aut et al. 2020).

R is available with no fee under the Free Software Foundation’s GNU General Public License. Because of this and its extensive, open-source and actively developed, package ecosystem, R is firmly situated as a top choice of programming languages.

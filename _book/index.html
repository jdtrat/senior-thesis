Hello, World

My name is Jonathan Trattner. In May 2021, I will graduate with a Bachelor’s of Science Degree in Computational Neuroscience from Wake Forest University. This website is the embodiment of my honor’s thesis, and it showcases a few of the exciting software tools I’ve developed during my time at Wake Forest. Projects that I’ve worked on this past year are used by over 3,000 people.

0.1 Foreword

Technically, Wake Forest does not have a major in Computational Neuroscience. Students who wish to study a topic not available through a specific department, though, may propose an Interdisciplinary Major. It’s not the most clear-cut process. Honestly, I would not recommend it for most people. There’s a lot of institutional red-tape to wade through. But, I am really glad that I did it.

0.2 Why did I major in Computational Neuroscience?

In high school, I read the book Thinking, Fast and Slow by Israeli Psychologist and Nobel Laureate Daniel Kahneman. It introduced me to the field of behavioral economics. At the time, I was taking AP Psychology and just got interested in the psychology of how people make decisions. When I got to Wake, I enrolled in Introduction to Neuroscience with the amazing Katy Lack. That class really whet my appetite for all things neuroscience, and I became interested in the five-year combined Bachelor’s/Master’s degree in Neuroscience that Wake offered. I met with the program director, Carol Milligan, and she introduced me to this newly hired professor, Ken Kishida. His lab uses behavioral tasks and neuroimaging methods to explore the neurophysiological mechanisms underlying decision-making. I’ve been working with Ken since spring 2018 and our work really inspired me to design my own major.

0.3 What am I doing with Computational Neuroscience?

While working with Ken on my master’s thesis, I developed an interest in the programming language R. I took a course in Statistical Learning with the brilliant Lucy D’Agostino McGowan and asked her if she would be my thesis advisor. Fortunately for me, she said yes. Working with her over the past year has been an incredible learning experience. Together

I’ve had the privilege of working with so many intelligent, kind, and talented people, and have had many wonderful role models to look up at. I’d like to make as my brilliant mentor, Lucy D’Agostino McGowan.
